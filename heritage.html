Our Story

Ninety Years of Welcoming the World

What began as a single palace on a hilltop has grown into a collection of palaces, resorts and city hotels, each restored stone by stone and run by families who have served three generations of guests.

We do not build hotels that imitate the past — we keep palaces alive, and let today's traveller live inside their history for a while.

Every restoration begins with the same question: what would the family who once lived here want a guest to feel, walking through this doorway? The answer shapes everything from the colour of the plaster to the placement of a single lamp in a courtyard.

Our Journey

A History Told in Chapters

From one hilltop palace to a nationwide collection — the milestones that shaped who we are.

1932

The First Palace Opens Its Doors

A royal hunting palace in the Aravalli hills is opened to travellers for the first time, under the care of the family that had lived there for four generations.

1958

A Second Generation Takes Over

The founder's daughter expands the collection with a lakeside property in Udaipur, setting the template for restoration over reconstruction.

1981

The Coastal Chapter Begins

A crumbling Portuguese-era estate in Goa is rescued and reopened as our first coastal resort, blending European and Konkani craftsmanship.

2004

Kaveri Palaces, As a Name

Eleven independently run properties are brought together under one name, without losing their individual character or local ownership.

2026

Thirty-One Addresses, One Promise

Today the collection spans fourteen states, from Himalayan retreats to backwater houseboats, each still run by the people who know it best.

Our Philosophy

Restoration, Not Reinvention

Three principles guide every property we take under our care.

Principle One

Keep the Building's Memory

Original frescoes, carved doorways and courtyards are preserved wherever possible, restored by the same families of artisans who maintain them generation after generation.

Principle Two

Employ From the Land

The vast majority of our staff are hired from the towns and villages surrounding each property, so guests are welcomed by people who genuinely know its history.

Principle Three

Comfort Without Compromise

Modern plumbing, climate control and connectivity are added discreetly, so the palace never stops feeling like a palace.
